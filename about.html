Statement

As a woman, I am accompanied, even when alone, by the image of my own self. I confront myself repeatedly in a refracted mirror of selves and images of selves - enviable and unenviable forms tumbling upon each other like commodities spit out at the end of a conveyor belt. I paint myself as a way to externalize this tumbling and, in so doing, to see it. What do many selfs wear and what pose do they assume when caught off guard, when the scrimmage between particles vying for space becomes fixed? I paint myself in order to find grounding in what is real and what is imagined, in traditions of representation, and traditions of looking and of being looked at.

Bio

Bronwyn Roe is a painter and video artist. Her work has been featured in the Tribeca Cinemas 2008 New York Arab & South Asian Film Festival, the 400th Anniversary of the Pontifical Academy of Sciences at the Vatican, the Pollack Krasner House, and the Montauk Lighthouse. She lives and works in NYC.
